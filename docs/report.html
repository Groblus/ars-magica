Executive Summary

Rules authority

Use reviewed/Ars Magica - Definitive Edition (Core Rules).md as the baseline. It is marked done, includes errata notes, has convenience links, indexes, a reference guide, and 16 major rules/setting chapters.

First-time path

Start with the Definitive core. Add Covenants early. Use True Lineages and Societates for most initial magus concepts and Hermetic politics.

Strong AI potential

The corpus is heading-rich and table-heavy. Best next step is a heading/chapter index, followed by citation-preserving chunks and structured extraction of spells, virtues, flaws, abilities, covenant options, story seeds, and stat blocks.

Outdated material

The 3e and 4e books are not first-pass rules sources. Treat them as legacy regional lore, scenario inspiration, and conversion targets. Do not surface their mechanics as current without warning.

Order of HermesTrue LineagesSocietatestribunal book

Goal: make the Order feel like an institution with laws, rivals, messengers, judges, factions, and consequences.

Creative Build Ideas

Corpus Index

Create ars-magica-corpus-index.json with title, edition, line count, completion state, headings, chapter spans, and source citations. This is the foundation for every later tool.

Citation RAG

Chunk by heading subtree rather than fixed token windows. Preserve book, edition, chapter, heading_path, line_start, and line_end.

Covenant Builder

Use Core plus Covenants to generate covenant dossiers: aura, boons/hooks, governance, resources, library, vis sources, labs, covenfolk, and seasonal story pressure.

Spell And Lab Tools

Extract spells, guidelines, techniques/forms, ranges/durations/targets, lab totals, enchantment rules, and seasonal activity checklists into fast lookup tools.

Tribunal Gazetteer

Extract places, covenants, magi, factions, names, languages, story hooks, and timelines from tribunal books. Add edition warnings to legacy regions.

Agent Skills

Create focused skills: rules guide, character helper, covenant builder, seasonal advancement assistant, Storyguide prep, tribunal explorer, and legacy conversion auditor.

Detailed Notes

What is outdated?

The 3e and 4e books are outdated for Definitive Edition rules use: Lion of the North, Tribunals of Hermes - Rome, Heirs to Merlin, Sanctuary of Ice, and Icelandic Wars. Keep their places, names, conflicts, and atmosphere; convert or ignore mechanics.

What is compatible but not core?

The 5e sourcebooks are generally useful with Definitive Edition, but they are optional. Do not make new players read all of them. Pull them in by player concept, chosen tribunal, or saga focus.

What makes this corpus agent-friendly?

The books are Markdown, heading-rich, and contain repeated structures such as tables, stat blocks, spell entries, powers, virtues/flaws, story seeds, and indexes. That makes them unusually good candidates for deterministic parsing plus LLM-assisted cleanup.
